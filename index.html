Reliability in the Workplace

Reliability is one of the most important qualities in any job. It helps build trust, improves teamwork, and ensures work is completed safely and efficiently.

1. What is Reliability?

Reliability means being dependable and consistent in your work. A reliable worker shows up on time, completes tasks properly, follows instructions, and can be trusted by supervisors and coworkers.

It also means taking responsibility for your actions and doing your job safely and correctly every time.

2. Examples of Reliability in the Workplace

Example 1: Showing Up on Time

A reliable worker arrives on time every day and is ready to work. This helps the team stay on schedule and prevents delays on the jobsite.

Example 2: Following Safety Procedures

A reliable worker always follows safety rules, uses proper equipment, and does not take shortcuts. This helps prevent accidents and keeps everyone safe.

3. Activity for the Class

Quick Group Activity

  • Work in small groups (2–3 people).
  • Discuss one situation where reliability is important at work.
  • Give one example of what happens if someone is NOT reliable.
  • Be ready to share your answer with the class.
