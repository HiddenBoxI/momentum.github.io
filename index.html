Reliability in the Workplace

Reliability, work ethic, and quality are three key traits that define how a person performs at work. Together, they determine whether someone is dependable, effective, and valuable to a team.

What is Reliability?

Reliability means being dependable and trustworthy. A reliable worker follows through on commitments, meets deadlines, communicates when issues arise, and maintains consistency over time.

Work Ethic vs Quality vs Reliability

Work Ethic

  • Showing up on time
  • Putting in consistent effort
  • Taking initiative
  • Staying focused
  • Being accountable

Quality

  • Attention to detail
  • Accuracy and neatness
  • Meeting standards
  • Doing work properly
  • Producing reliable results

Reliability

  • Following through on commitments
  • Meeting deadlines
  • Being dependable
  • Communicating issues
  • Consistency over time

How They Work Together

Work ethic drives how hard you try.

Quality reflects how well you do the work.

Reliability shows whether people can count on you every time.

When all three are strong, you become a highly valuable worker in any workplace.

Activity: Who Would You Hire?

Quick Discussion (2–3 minutes)

  • Person A: Always on time, works hard, but makes mistakes
  • Person B: Does excellent work, but is often late
  • Question: Who would you hire and why?

If I had to choose, I would choose Person A.

Work ethic and reliability are harder to teach, while quality can be improved through training and experience. A dependable worker can grow, but an unreliable worker can negatively affect the entire team.
